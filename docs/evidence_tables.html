Aasbo
2002

 60

Include: Patients aged 18-80 years; ASA physical status I-III.
Exclude: Known allergy or contraindications to any of the study drugs; history of psychiatric disease.

Patients in the regional anesthesia had less pain postop at 1 h (Regional M=0.41, SD = 0.50 vs General M=1.37, SD=0.85; P = <0.001), 2 h (Regional M=0.53, SD = 0.51 vs General M=1.0, SD=0.5; P = 0.02), 24 h rest (Regional M=0.17, SD = 0.46 vs General M = 0.50, SD = 0.63; P = 0.01), 48 h rest (Regional M=0.07, SD = 0.25 vs General M = 0.29, SD=0.46; P = <0.001), 48 h activity (Regional M = 0.73, SD = 0.74 vs General M = 1.24, SD = 0.74; P = 0.01), 7 days rest (Regional M = 0.07, SD = 0.37 vs General M = 0.25, SD = 0.44; P = 0.005). Patients in the regional anesthesia also had lower analgesic consumption postop for codeine 6-24 h (Regional M = 60, SD = 57 vs General M = 90, SD = 57; P = <0.05), codeine 24-48 h (Regional M = 18, SD = 23 vs General M = 43.4, SD = 49.8; P = <0.03), codeine 48h-7 days (Regional M = 26.9, SD = 58 vs General M = 61.3, SD = 101; P = <0.007), and Ketobemidone total (Regional M = 0.03, SD = 0.18 vs General M = 2.57, SD = 3.9; P = <0.001). Lastly, patients in the regional group had higher patient satisfaction postop at 48h (Regional M=3.0, SD = 0 vs General M=2.68, SD=0.48; P = <0.001) and 7 days (Regional M=2.96, SD = 0.19 vs General M=2.46, SD=0.59; P = <0.001).
